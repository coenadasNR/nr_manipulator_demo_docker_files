Pick and Place Demo

This project is for the National Robotarium (NR) Manipulator Setup, featuring Demo 1 - a pick and place task. The demo showcases the integration of robotic manipulation with digital twin technology, utilizing the following key components:

  • xArm5 Robotic Arm: A 5-axis robotic manipulator used for the physical pick and place operations.
  • MoveIt 2: An advanced motion planning framework for robotic applications, employed to control the xArm5's movements.
  • Isaac Sim: NVIDIA's physics-based simulation environment, used to create a digital twin of the robotic setup for testing and visualization.
  • ROS 2: The Robot Operating System (version 2) serves as the underlying software framework, facilitating communication between different components of the system.
The demo aims to demonstrate the capabilities of the xArm5 in performing precise pick and place tasks, while showcasing the benefits of using a digital twin for development and testing. This setup allows for seamless transition between simulated and real-world environments, enhancing the development process and reducing potential risks during implementation.
